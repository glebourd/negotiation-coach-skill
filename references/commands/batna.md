# batna [negotiation] — Deep BATNA Analysis

## Purpose
Deep BATNA analysis for one or both sides. The BATNA is the source of power in any negotiation — weak BATNAs should be strengthened before entering, not just identified and accepted.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Your BATNA
- What specifically happens if no deal is reached?
- Is this alternative genuinely available, or hypothetical?
- How attractive is it on an absolute basis?
- What would it cost (time, money, relationship, opportunity) to execute?
- **Is there room to strengthen it before the negotiation?** This is the most underused lever — a stronger BATNA improves every outcome and changes the psychological dynamic from the first moment.

Rate BATNA strength: **Strong / Moderate / Weak / Unknown**

### Step 2: Derive Reservation Price
- What's the worst deal you would accept over executing the BATNA?
- Adjust for: relationship value, ego, switching costs, strategic importance beyond immediate terms
- Is the RP quantified, or just a vague sense? Push to quantify. Vague RPs get violated under pressure.
- Flag if the user is conflating BATNA with RP — they are different things. BATNA is what you do if there's no deal. RP is the worst deal you'd accept over that.

### Step 3: Counterpart's BATNA
- What do they do if no deal is reached?
- How attractive is their alternative?
- What constraints might limit their ability to execute it (timing, cost, availability, relationships)?
- Are there ways to weaken their BATNA or make the deal more attractive by comparison?

Rate counterpart BATNA strength: **Strong / Moderate / Weak / Unknown**

### Step 4: Power Diagnosis
Compare both BATNAs:
- Who needs this deal more?
- What does this imply for anchoring strategy and concession pacing?
- If your BATNA is weak and counterpart's is strong: either strengthen your BATNA before negotiating, or accept limited leverage and focus on understanding their interests to find trades they value highly.
- If your BATNA is strong: lean into it — higher aspirations, more patience on concessions, credible willingness to walk.

### Step 5: BATNA Improvement Plan (if weak)
If the BATNA is weak, generate 2-3 specific actions to strengthen it before the negotiation:
- Develop alternative options (competing offers, other buyers, other vendors)
- Create competitive pressure (let it be known you're evaluating alternatives)
- Improve the status quo alternative (what makes staying put more acceptable?)
- Build credibility of the walk-away threat (a weak BATNA you've never exercised isn't credible; building a track record of walking when needed strengthens every future negotiation)

## Output
- BATNA assessment for both sides with strength rating
- Derived RP with reasoning
- Power diagnosis and strategic implication
- BATNA improvement plan if applicable
